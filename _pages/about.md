## Mechanical Engineer specialized in Mechatronics and Robotics

I am a Mechanical Engineer currently pursuing a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering at **Politecnico di Milano**, with a specialization in **Mechatronics and Robotics**. My academic background has provided me with a solid foundation in mechanics, automation, control systems, and robotic technologies, while also strengthening my analytical approach to engineering problems and system design.

My main interests lie in the development of **robotic and mechatronic systems**, with particular attention to the integration of **control, automation, simulation, and intelligent decision-making methods** into structured engineering workflows. I am especially interested in solutions that connect rigorous engineering principles with practical implementation, from mechanical design and dynamic modeling to task planning and advanced automation architectures.

This website collects a selection of my academic and technical work, including projects related to robotics, control, simulation, and intelligent automation. It is intended as a concise overview of my background, technical interests, and current development path.

## Selected Projects

### Master’s Thesis – Multi-Agent Framework for Natural Language-Based Human-Robot Collaboration

Development of a **configuration-driven multi-agent framework** for robotic task planning and execution starting from natural language instructions. The project focuses on structuring the interaction between high-level language understanding and deterministic robotic execution, with particular attention to planning consistency, parameter extraction, scene updates, and validation before physical execution.

This work combines architectural design, modular software organization, and experimentation on integrated robotic workflows involving instruction interpretation, action planning, and execution supervision.

### Bayesian Optimization for Robot Control and Parameter Estimation

Development of an optimization-based approach for improving robotic control performance through the use of **Bayesian Optimization**. The project addressed the tuning of control parameters and the estimation of relevant physical quantities in order to improve trajectory tracking and dynamic compensation while limiting the computational cost associated with repeated simulations.

The work involved control-oriented modeling, simulation-driven optimization, and performance evaluation under different parameter configurations.

### Mechanical Design of a 4-DOF Robotic Manipulator

Design and modeling of a **4-degree-of-freedom robotic manipulator**, with focus on mechanical architecture, joint arrangement, and the corresponding kinematic and dynamic implications of design choices. The activity included conceptual development, CAD-based design, and analysis of the manipulator structure in relation to motion capabilities and system behavior.

This project strengthened my interest in the connection between mechanical design decisions and the resulting control and motion characteristics of robotic systems.
